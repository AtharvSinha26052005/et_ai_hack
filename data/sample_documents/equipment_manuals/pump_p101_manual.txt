1. GENERAL DESCRIPTION
======================
The Grundfos CR 64-3-2 is a vertical, multistage centrifugal pump designed for clean water and low-viscosity liquid transfer applications. The pump is equipped with a cartridge-type mechanical seal for reliable leak-free operation.

Key Features:
- Vertical inline configuration reduces floor space requirement
- Stainless steel (SS316) construction for corrosion resistance
- Cartridge mechanical seal for easy maintenance
- Dynamically balanced impellers for low vibration operation
- Standard IEC motor mounting for easy motor replacement

3. INSTALLATION REQUIREMENTS
==============================
3.1 Foundation
- Concrete foundation minimum 150 mm above floor level
- Foundation bolt size: M20 x 4 nos.
- Foundation mass: minimum 3x pump weight (1,200 kg concrete)
- Grouting: Epoxy grout (Masterflow 648 or equivalent)
- Level tolerance: ±0.1 mm/m in both directions

3.2 Piping
- Suction piping: Minimum one size larger than pump suction (DN 125 recommended)
- Suction piping must slope upward toward pump to avoid air pockets
- Eccentric reducer at pump suction (flat side on top)
- Minimum 5D straight length before pump suction
- Isolation valves: Gate type on suction, Globe type on discharge
- Check valve on discharge to prevent reverse flow
- Pressure gauge tappings on suction and discharge flanges
- Flexible connections recommended to absorb piping stress

3.3 Electrical
- Motor supply: 415V ± 10%, 3-Phase, 50 Hz
- Cable size: 4C x 35 mm² Cu (for 55 kW at 415V)
- Overload relay setting: 105 A (Full Load Current: 95 A)
- Motor protection: MPCB + Contactor + Overload Relay
- Emergency stop: Mushroom-head push button at pump location
- Phase rotation: Check correct rotation direction before coupling

4. OPERATING PROCEDURES
========================
4.1 Pre-Start Checks
□ Verify suction valve is FULLY OPEN
□ Verify discharge valve is CLOSED (for low-power starting)
□ Check pump is primed (suction line and casing filled with liquid)
□ Verify coupling guard is installed
□ Check oil level in bearing housing (SAE 46 oil, mid-sight glass)
□ Verify all isolation blinds removed from suction/discharge
□ Verify cooling water to seal/bearing (if applicable)
□ Check motor terminal box cover is closed
□ Record suction pressure gauge reading: _____ bar
□ Verify no maintenance permits outstanding for P-101A/B

4.2 Normal Startup
1. Ensure all pre-start checks completed
2. Start motor from local control station or DCS
3. Observe ammeter — current should spike and settle to normal within 5 seconds
4. Gradually OPEN discharge valve over 30-60 seconds
5. Monitor:
   - Discharge pressure: should reach 12-14 bar
   - Motor current: should be 60-85 A at rated flow
   - Vibration: should be < 4.5 mm/s RMS
   - Seal: no visible leakage (max 2-3 drops per minute during break-in)
   - Bearing temperature: should stabilize below 75°C within 30 minutes
6. Record all operating parameters on startup log sheet

4.3 Normal Shutdown
1. Gradually CLOSE discharge valve over 30-60 seconds
2. Stop motor
3. CLOSE suction valve ONLY if pump will be isolated for maintenance
4. Record operating parameters at shutdown

4.4 Emergency Shutdown
1. Press emergency stop button at pump location OR trip from DCS
2. Do NOT close valves — let pump coast to stop
3. Notify shift supervisor immediately
4. Record circumstances leading to emergency shutdown
5. Do NOT restart until cause is investigated and cleared

6. TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE
=========================
Symptom                         Probable Cause                  Corrective Action
-------                         --------------                  -----------------
No discharge / low head         Air in suction line             Vent pump casing, check suction pipe joints
                                Suction strainer blocked        Clean strainer (check DP across strainer)
                                Impeller wear                   Replace impeller and wear rings
                                Wrong rotation                  Swap any two motor phases

High vibration                  Misalignment                    Laser alignment (target: 0.05/0.03 mm)
                                Bearing wear                    Replace bearings
                                Cavitation                      Check NPSHA, clean suction strainer
                                Foundation loose                Re-torque foundation bolts
                                Impeller imbalance              Balance or replace impeller

Excessive seal leakage          Dry running damage              Replace mechanical seal
                                Shaft runout > 0.05 mm          Machine or replace shaft
                                Wrong seal face combination     Use SiC/SiC for abrasive fluids

Motor overheating               Overload (high flow)            Throttle discharge, check for open bypass
                                Voltage imbalance               Check supply voltage (max 2% imbalance)
                                Blocked ventilation             Clean motor cooling fins/fan cover

High bearing temperature        Over-greasing                   Follow greasing chart exactly
                                Wrong lubricant                 Use only recommended grade
                                Bearing damage                  Replace bearing
