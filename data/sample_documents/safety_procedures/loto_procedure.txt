LOCKOUT/TAGOUT (LOTO) PROCEDURE
================================
Document No: SOP-LOTO-001
Revision: Rev 4
Effective Date: 2024-04-01
Next Review: 2025-04-01
Approved By: Vikram Reddy (Plant Manager)

1. PURPOSE
==========
This procedure establishes requirements for the lockout/tagout (LOTO) of energy
sources to ensure that equipment and machinery are properly isolated and
de-energized before maintenance, servicing, or inspection activities. This
procedure is mandatory for all personnel performing work that requires isolation
of hazardous energy sources.

2. SCOPE
========
This procedure applies to:
- All rotating equipment (pumps, compressors, motors, fans)
- All pressure equipment (vessels, heat exchangers, piping systems)
- Electrical equipment (switchgear, MCCs, transformers, UPS systems)
- Automated valves and control systems
- Hydraulic and pneumatic systems
- Thermal energy sources (steam, hot oil, hot water)
- Chemical energy sources (process fluids, hazardous materials)

Exceptions:
- Minor servicing that does not require removal of guards or exposure to
  hazardous energy (e.g., minor adjustments, filter changes with block valves
  already isolated) — requires supervisor approval
- Hot tap operations under approved procedure SOP-HOT-TAP-001

3. REFERENCES
=============
- OISD-STD-105: Work Permit System
- Factory Act 1948, Section 21: Fencing of Machinery
- IS 3646 Part 1: Code of Practice for Interior Illumination (emergency)
- ISO 45001: Occupational Health & Safety Management Systems

4. DEFINITIONS
==============
Lockout:    Placement of a lock on an energy-isolating device to ensure that
            the device cannot be operated until the lock is removed.
Tagout:     Placement of a tag on an energy-isolating device to indicate that
            the device must not be operated until the tag is removed.
Affected Employee: An employee who operates or uses equipment on which servicing
            or maintenance is being performed under LOTO.
Authorized Employee: An employee who locks out/tags out equipment to perform
            servicing or maintenance. Must be trained and certified.
Energy Isolating Device: A mechanical device that physically prevents the
            transmission or release of energy (e.g., circuit breaker, block
            valve, blind flange, disconnect switch).

    - Maintain LOTO training records

    - Authorize LOTO application
    - Verify energy isolation before work begins
    - Coordinate LOTO removal after work completion
    - Maintain LOTO log register

5.4 Authorized Employees (All maintenance technicians)
    - Apply personal locks and tags
    - Verify zero energy state before starting work
    - Remove own locks upon work completion
    - NEVER operate equipment under LOTO

    - Investigate LOTO violations
    - Maintain incident records

6. ENERGY ISOLATION PROCEDURES
================================

6.1 STEP 1: PREPARATION
   a. Identify all energy sources for the equipment:
      □ Electrical (motor power, control power, UPS backup)
      □ Mechanical (stored energy — springs, counterweights)
      □ Hydraulic (pressurized oil lines)
      □ Pneumatic (instrument air, plant air)
      □ Thermal (steam, hot oil, hot surfaces)
      □ Chemical (process fluids, hazardous materials)
      □ Gravitational (suspended loads, elevated platforms)
   b. Identify all energy-isolating devices (refer to equipment isolation
      schematic — available in DCS/control room)
   c. Obtain Work Permit (SOP-PTW-001) — signed by area authority
   d. Notify all affected employees

6.2 STEP 2: SHUTDOWN
   a. Follow normal shutdown procedure for the equipment
      - Pumps: SOP-PUMP-STOP-001
      - Compressors: SOP-COMP-START-001 (shutdown section)
      - Boilers: SOP-BOILER-STOP-001
   b. Bring equipment to zero energy state
   c. Confirm shutdown with DCS operator (EMP-018 / EMP-034)

6.3 STEP 3: ISOLATION
   a. Operate each energy-isolating device to the ISOLATED position:
      - Electrical: Open circuit breaker, rack out to TEST position, remove
        fuses where applicable
      - Mechanical: Close and lock block valves on suction and discharge.
        Install spectacle blinds (SOP-BLINDING-001) for positive isolation
      - Pneumatic: Close instrument air supply valve, bleed air from actuators
      - Thermal: Close steam/hot oil isolation valves, install blinds if
        temperature > 60°C
   b. Verify isolation:
      - Attempt to start equipment from LOCAL control station — must NOT start
      - Check pressure gauges read ZERO on isolated sections
      - Check temperature within acceptable limits for work
      - For electrical: Use voltage tester to confirm DEAD at equipment terminals

6.4 STEP 4: LOCKOUT & TAGOUT
   a. Apply PERSONAL lock (red padlock with name tag) on each energy-isolating
      device
   b. Each worker applies their OWN lock — no sharing of locks
   c. Attach DANGER tag with:
      - Name of authorized employee
      - Date and time of lockout
      - Reason for lockout
      - Expected completion time
      - Work permit number
   d. Keys remain with the INDIVIDUAL who applied the lock — no key box
   e. For group lockout: Group LOTO coordinator applies master lock. Each
      individual then applies personal lock to group lockbox.

6.5 STEP 5: VERIFY ZERO ENERGY STATE
   a. After lockout, attempt to restart equipment from DCS — must fail
   b. Verify all stored energy is dissipated:
      - Pressure gauges at zero
      - Springs/counterweights blocked
      - Capacitors discharged
      - Elevated components supported/blocked
   c. Only after verification can work begin

6.6 STEP 6: PERFORM WORK
   a. Proceed with maintenance/servicing activity
   b. If additional energy sources are discovered during work, STOP work
      and apply additional LOTO before continuing

6.7 STEP 7: RESTORATION
   a. Work complete — area cleaned, tools accounted for
   b. All guards and safety devices reinstalled
   c. Notify all affected employees that LOTO will be removed
   d. Verify all personnel clear of equipment
   e. Remove personal locks and tags — each person removes ONLY their own lock
   f. Restore energy-isolating devices to NORMAL position
   g. Remove blinds (record in blind register)
   h. Test equipment (trial run) under controlled conditions
   i. Return equipment to operations — close work permit

7. SPECIAL SITUATIONS
=====================
7.1 Shift Change During LOTO
    - Outgoing shift worker removes their lock
    - Incoming shift worker immediately applies their lock
    - Transfer must be direct — no gap between locks
    - Document transfer in LOTO register

7.2 Lost Key / Absent Employee
    - Only Plant Manager can authorize lock cutting
    - Requires physical verification that employee is NOT in/on equipment
    - Two-person witness for lock cutting
    - Replacement lock applied immediately
    - Incident report filed

7.3 Group Lockout (More than 6 workers)
    - LOTO coordinator designated
    - Group lockbox with individual locks
    - Coordinator maintains master log
    - ALL individual locks must be removed before master lock is removed

8. TRAINING REQUIREMENTS
=========================
- Initial LOTO training: 8 hours (before any maintenance work assignment)
- Annual refresher: 4 hours
- Retraining after any LOTO violation or near-miss
- Training records maintained by HR and Safety Department

9. VIOLATION CONSEQUENCES
==========================
- First violation: Written warning + retraining
- Second violation: 1-week suspension + retraining
- Third violation: Termination
- Any violation that results in injury: Immediate investigation per
  SOP-INCIDENT-001, potential termination and legal action
