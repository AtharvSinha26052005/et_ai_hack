1. PURPOSE
==========
This Emergency Response Plan (ERP) establishes procedures for responding to fire
emergencies at the IntelliPlant industrial facility. It defines roles, responsibilities,
communication protocols, and action steps to minimize injury, loss of life, property
damage, and environmental impact during a fire event.

2. SCOPE
========
This ERP covers fire emergencies including:
- Process area fires (hydrocarbon, chemical)
- Electrical fires (transformers, switchgear, cable galleries)
- Storage tank fires (atmospheric and pressure vessels)
- Building fires (control room, admin, workshop)
- Vehicle fires (loading/unloading areas)

4. EMERGENCY CLASSIFICATION
=============================

Level 1 — MINOR (Local Emergency)
   Definition: Small fire that can be controlled by area personnel using
   portable extinguishers. No threat to personnel or equipment outside
   immediate area.
   Actions: Area response only. No plant alarm. Report to shift supervisor.
   Example: Small spill fire, trash fire, electrical short circuit.

Level 2 — MAJOR (Site Emergency)
   Definition: Fire that requires fire team response and may threaten adjacent
   areas. Potential for escalation. Process area involvement.
   Actions: Fire alarm sounded. Fire team mobilized. Non-essential personnel
   evacuate to assembly points. Mutual aid on standby.
   Example: Pump seal fire, heat exchanger leak fire, transformer fire.

Level 3 — DISASTER (Off-Site Emergency)
   Definition: Large-scale fire with potential off-site impact. Tank fire,
   major unit fire, multiple fire fronts.
   Actions: Full plant evacuation. Mutual aid activated. Government agencies
   notified (DISH, PESO, Factories Inspector). Community warning.
   Example: Tank farm fire, uncontrolled process fire, explosion.

5. FIRE DETECTION & ALARM SYSTEM
==================================
Detection Equipment:
- UV/IR flame detectors in process areas (77 units)
- Heat detectors in electrical rooms and cable galleries (42 units)
- Smoke detectors in control room, admin building (28 units)
- Manual call points at strategic locations (32 units)
- CCTV coverage of tank farm, process areas, loading bay

Alarm Sequence:
1. Fire detected → Fire alarm panel in control room activates
2. DCS operator verifies alarm (CCTV/field check) within 60 seconds
3. If confirmed fire:
   - Continuous siren (3 minutes) for Level 2/3
   - PA announcement: "ATTENTION: FIRE ALARM — [LOCATION]. ALL PERSONNEL
     PROCEED TO ASSEMBLY POINTS. FIRE TEAM REPORT TO [LOCATION]."
4. All-clear: Intermittent siren (30 seconds on, 30 seconds off, 3 cycles)

6. FIREFIGHTING SYSTEMS
=========================

6.1 Fire Water System
   - Fire water tank: V-501 (500 m³ capacity, 4-hour supply at design rate)
   - Fire water pumps:
     * P-501 (Jockey): 15 kW, auto-start on pressure drop below 7 bar
     * P-502 (Main Electric): 125 kW, auto-start on pressure drop below 6 bar
     * P-503 (Main Diesel): 125 kW, auto-start on pressure drop below 5 bar
   - Fire water ring main: 250 mm dia, looped around plant
   - Design flow rate: 125 m³/hr at 7 bar residual pressure
   - Hydrant spacing: maximum 45 m (per OISD-STD-116)
   - Total hydrants: 24 (double-headed, with 63mm outlets)

6.2 Foam System
   - Foam concentrate storage: 10,000 liters (3% AFFF)
   - Fixed foam pourers on storage tanks V-301, V-302 (2 per tank)
   - Foam monitors at tank farm (4 units, 1900 LPM each)
   - Portable foam equipment: 4 x proportioners with 200L foam drums

6.3 Deluge Systems
   - V-201 (Reactor): Medium velocity water spray (MVWS), auto-actuated on
     fusible plug/manual
   - T-201 (Column): MVWS on lower 5 meters
   - C-201, C-202 (Compressors): MVWS coverage
   - TR-101 (Transformer): Dedicated deluge system

6.4 Portable Equipment
   - DCP extinguishers: 120 units (9 kg) throughout plant
   - CO2 extinguishers: 40 units (6.5 kg) in electrical areas
   - Foam trolley units: 4 (50 liter capacity)
   - Fire hose boxes: 24 (with 63mm x 15m lay-flat hose + branch pipe)

7. EVACUATION PROCEDURES
==========================

7.1 Assembly Points
   Assembly Point A: Main gate parking area (for Admin, Stores, Workshop)
   Assembly Point B: North boundary open area (for Unit-1, Unit-2, Utility)
   Assembly Point C: East boundary road (for Unit-3, Tank Farm, Loading Bay)
   Wind sock locations: 4 (one at each boundary corner)
   Rule: Evacuate UPWIND and CROSSWIND from fire. Check wind direction.

7.2 Evacuation Routes
   - Primary escape routes marked with photoluminescent signs
   - Emergency exits: minimum 2 per building, outward-opening
   - Emergency lighting: battery-backed (30-minute minimum)
   - Stairways: fireproof enclosures with self-closing doors
   - Assembly point signs: illuminated, visible from 50m

7.3 Headcount
   - All personnel must report to assembly point within 5 minutes
   - Area wardens conduct headcount using daily attendance register
   - Report headcount to Safety Coordinator within 10 minutes
   - Any missing person: initiate search and rescue protocol

8. EMERGENCY SHUTDOWN PROCEDURES
==================================
Level 2/3 fire may require:
a. Emergency shutdown of affected unit(s)
b. DCS operator executes ESD from control room
c. Manual field actions per unit-specific ESD checklist
d. Priorities:
   1. STOP feeds to fire-affected equipment
   2. DEPRESSURIZE vessels via blowdown system
   3. ISOLATE fuel sources (block valves, fire-safe SDVs)
   4. MAINTAIN cooling water to non-affected equipment
   5. STEAM curtain activation around fire perimeter

10. POST-EMERGENCY ACTIONS
============================
a. Fire-out confirmed by Fire Team Leader
b. Cooling continued for minimum 6 hours for hydrocarbon fires
c. Area declared safe for re-entry by Safety Officer (gas-free certificate)
d. Incident investigation initiated within 24 hours (SOP-INCIDENT-001)
e. Regulatory reporting: within 4 hours (fatal), 12 hours (serious)
f. Insurance notification: within 24 hours
g. Lessons learned shared within 1 week
