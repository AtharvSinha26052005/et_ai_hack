OISD-STD-105: WORK PERMIT SYSTEM
==================================
Oil Industry Safety Directorate (OISD)
Ministry of Petroleum & Natural Gas, Government of India
Standard on Work Permit System in Petroleum Industry Installations

Version: 2024 (Fifth Revision)
Effective From: 2024-01-01

DISCLAIMER: This document is a summary/excerpt for training and compliance
reference purposes. For the official and complete standard, refer to the
original document available from OISD (www.oisd.gov.in).

1. SCOPE
========
This standard is applicable to all petroleum industry installations including:
- Refineries
- Gas processing plants
- Petrochemical plants
- Oil and gas exploration and production installations
- Petroleum storage terminals and depots
- Cross-country pipelines and associated facilities
- LPG bottling plants
- Marketing installations

2. PURPOSE
==========
To establish a uniform system of work permits to ensure that:
(a) All work involving potential hazards is properly authorized
(b) Adequate safety precautions are taken before, during, and after the work
(c) Work is coordinated with operating activities
(d) Communication between all involved parties is established and maintained

3. TYPES OF WORK PERMITS
=========================

3.1 Cold Work Permit (CWP)
   Required for: Work that does not involve a source of ignition or opening of
   hydrocarbon-containing equipment.
   Examples: Painting, scaffolding, insulation work, instrument calibration,
   equipment inspection, civil work in non-hazardous areas.
   Validity: Maximum 7 days (shift-wise endorsement required)

3.2 Hot Work Permit (HWP)
   Required for: Any work involving a source of ignition or that could generate
   sufficient heat, spark, or flame to ignite flammable vapors.
   Examples: Welding, cutting, grinding, drilling, use of non-explosion-proof
   electrical equipment in hazardous areas.
   Validity: Maximum one shift (8 hours). Fresh permit required for each shift.
   Special Requirements:
   - Continuous gas testing during work (LEL monitoring)
   - Fire watch during work and for 30 minutes after completion
   - Fire extinguisher at work site (minimum 2 x 9 kg DCP)
   - Hot work restricted within 15m of flammable material storage

3.3 Confined Space Entry Permit (CSEP)
   Required for: Entry into any space not designed for continuous human occupancy
   with limited access/egress and potential for hazardous atmosphere.
   Examples: Vessels, tanks, columns, heat exchanger shells, manholes, pits,
   tunnels, large diameter pipes.
   Special Requirements:
   - Atmospheric testing: O2 (19.5-23.5%), LEL (<1%), H2S (<10 ppm), CO (<25 ppm)
   - Continuous atmospheric monitoring during entry
   - Rescue standby team with SCBA and retrieval equipment
   - Entry/exit log maintained at entry point
   - Maximum entry duration: 2 hours (reassessment required for extension)
   - Communication system between entrant and standby person

3.4 Electrical Work Permit (EWP)
   Required for: All electrical work on systems rated above 250V or in
   hazardous areas.
   Examples: Cable jointing, switchgear maintenance, motor replacement,
   transformer work, UPS battery work.
   Special Requirements:
   - Electrical isolation verified by qualified electrician
   - LOTO applied (refer SOP-LOTO-001)
   - Voltage testing (prove dead) before work commences
   - Grounding of isolated circuits where applicable

3.5 Excavation Permit (EP)
   Required for: Any digging, trenching, or excavation work.
   Special Requirements:
   - Underground services survey (cables, pipes, drains) before excavation
   - Depth > 1.2m requires shoring/bracing
   - Safe access/egress for depths > 1.5m (ladder at max 8m intervals)
   - Barriers and warning signs around excavation
   - Atmospheric monitoring if within 15m of hydrocarbon facility

3.6 Radiography Permit (RP)
   Required for: Any radiographic testing (RT) using gamma or X-ray sources.
   Special Requirements:
   - AERB-approved radiographer with valid license
   - Cordoned area with minimum 30m radius
   - Radiation survey after completion
   - Work scheduled during low-activity periods (night shift preferred)

4. PERMIT AUTHORIZATION LEVELS
================================
Level 1 (Area Operator):    Cold work in non-hazardous areas
Level 2 (Shift Supervisor): Cold work in hazardous areas, simple electrical work
Level 3 (Area In-Charge):   Hot work, confined space entry, complex electrical
Level 4 (Plant Manager):    Work near live hydrocarbons, multiple simultaneous
                            hazardous activities, radiography

5. PERMIT PROCEDURE
====================

5.1 Permit Request
   a. Work requestor (Maintenance/Contractor) fills Section A:
      - Description of work
      - Exact location (unit, area, equipment tag)
      - Tools and equipment to be used
      - Estimated duration
      - Number of workers

5.2 Hazard Assessment (by Permit Issuer)
   a. Identify all hazards at work site
   b. Review recent process conditions
   c. Check DCS for alarms, abnormal conditions
   d. Identify required precautions:
      □ Isolation required (electrical, mechanical, process)
      □ Gas testing required (initial + continuous)
      □ PPE requirements (standard + additional)
      □ Fire watch required
      □ Rescue standby required
      □ Environmental precautions (drip trays, bunding)

5.3 Pre-Work Site Visit
   a. Permit issuer and work requestor visit work site together
   b. Verify conditions match permit requirements
   c. Agree on safety precautions
   d. Identify emergency exits and assembly points

5.4 Permit Issuance
   a. All sections completed and signed
   b. Precautions verified in place
   c. Workers briefed on permit conditions
   d. Permit displayed at work site
   e. Control room informed

5.5 Work Execution
   a. Work proceeds under permit conditions
   b. Any change in conditions → STOP work, contact permit issuer
   c. Gas testing continued as specified
   d. Shift change: permit re-endorsed by incoming shift supervisor

5.6 Permit Closure
   a. Work completed, area cleaned
   b. All tools and materials removed
   c. Safety devices reinstalled
   d. Isolation removed (LOTO cleared)
   e. Work requestor signs Section C (work completion)
   f. Permit issuer verifies site condition
   g. Permit returned to control room for filing (retain for 1 year)

6. GAS TESTING REQUIREMENTS
============================
Equipment: Calibrated portable gas detector (4-gas: O2, LEL, H2S, CO)
Calibration: Monthly with certified calibration gas

Parameter    Acceptable Range    Action if Outside Range
---------    ----------------    ----------------------
O2           19.5% - 23.5%      Ventilate, investigate source
LEL          < 1% of LEL        Do not proceed, ventilate
H2S          < 10 ppm           Evacuate, don SCBA
CO           < 25 ppm           Increase ventilation

Testing Frequency:
- Initial test: before work starts
- Hot work: continuous during work
- Confined space: continuous during entry
- Cold work in hazardous area: every 2 hours

7. PPE REQUIREMENTS
====================
Standard PPE (all work permits):
- Safety helmet (IS 2925)
- Safety shoes (IS 15298)
- Safety goggles (IS 5983)
- Cotton overalls (no synthetic clothing in process areas)

Additional PPE (as specified on permit):
- Face shield — for grinding, chipping
- Welding helmet — for arc welding
- Fire-retardant clothing — for hot work
- Chemical-resistant gloves — for chemical handling
- SCBA — for H2S areas, emergency rescue
- Fall arrest harness — for work above 2m height
- Ear plugs/muffs — for areas exceeding 85 dB(A)

8. CONTRACTOR MANAGEMENT
==========================
All contractor personnel must:
a. Complete safety induction (minimum 4 hours)
b. Pass medical fitness test (for confined space work: special medical)
c. Possess valid trade license/certification
d. Carry photo ID at all times
e. Attend daily toolbox talk before work
f. Report to site safety officer for daily headcount

9. AUDIT AND REVIEW
=====================
a. Daily: Random permit checks by shift supervisor (minimum 2 per shift)
b. Weekly: Safety officer reviews all closed permits for completeness
c. Monthly: HSE committee reviews permit statistics and trends
d. Annual: Full procedure review and update
e. After any incident: Immediate review of relevant permit(s)

========================

- Show-cause notice from OISD
- Insurance claim rejection
