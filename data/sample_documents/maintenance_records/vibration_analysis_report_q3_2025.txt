==================================================

   Zone A (New/Reconditioned):     < 2.3 mm/s RMS
   Zone B (Acceptable for operation): 2.3 — 4.5 mm/s RMS
   Zone C (Limited operation):       4.5 — 7.1 mm/s RMS
   Zone D (Damage occurring):       > 7.1 mm/s RMS

   ALERT:  > 4.5 mm/s (increase monitoring frequency to weekly)

4.1 P-302 — SLOP OIL PUMP (ZONE D — ALARM)
   Equipment: Seepex BN 35-12 Progressive Cavity Pump, 7.5 kW
   Location: Unit-3 / ETP / Pump House

   Vibration Trend (DE Horizontal, mm/s RMS):
   Jan-25: 2.1 | Feb-25: 2.4 | Mar-25: 3.0 | Apr-25: 3.5 | May-25: 4.2 |
   Jun-25: 5.1 | Jul-25: 6.3 | Aug-25: 7.8 | Sep-25: 8.4

   Spectrum Analysis:
   - Dominant frequency: 1x RPM (1470 CPM) with high amplitude (6.2 mm/s)
   - Sub-synchronous component at 0.43x RPM observed
   - No bearing defect frequencies detected

   Diagnosis: Progressive rotor/stator wear (typical failure mode for PC pumps).
   The sub-synchronous frequency is characteristic of stator elastomer degradation.
   Current rate of increase suggests stator failure within 4-6 weeks.

   RECOMMENDATION: Schedule stator replacement within 2 weeks. Order: Seepex
   stator assembly P/N BN35-12-NR (NBR elastomer). Estimated downtime: 8 hours.
   Priority: HIGH
   Work Order Raised: WO-2025-0412 (assigned to Suresh Patel, EMP-002)

4.2 P-201A — COOLING WATER PUMP A (ZONE C — ALERT)
   Equipment: KSB Etanorm 100-200, 90 kW
   Location: Unit-2 / Cooling Tower / Pump Bay

   Vibration Trend (DE Horizontal, mm/s RMS):
   Jan-25: 3.2 | Feb-25: 3.1 | Mar-25: 3.4 | Apr-25: 3.6 | May-25: 4.0 |
   Jun-25: 4.3 | Jul-25: 4.5 | Aug-25: 4.8 | Sep-25: 5.2

   Spectrum Analysis:
   - Dominant frequency: 2x RPM (2970 CPM) — indicative of misalignment
   - Axial vibration: 3.8 mm/s (elevated relative to radial)
   - Phase analysis: 180° phase difference between DE and NDE horizontal —
     confirms angular misalignment
   - No bearing defect frequencies
   - Bearing temperature: DE 72°C (elevated but within alarm at 85°C)

   Diagnosis: Angular misalignment between pump and motor shafts. Likely cause:
   thermal growth differential or foundation settlement. Rate of change is slow —
   not immediately critical but action needed within 1 month.

   RECOMMENDATION: Schedule laser alignment check during next available window.
   If misalignment confirmed > 0.05mm angular, re-align per SOP-ALIGNMENT-001.
   Estimated downtime: 4-6 hours (requires switchover to P-201B standby).
   Priority: MEDIUM
   Work Order Raised: WO-2025-0418 (assigned to Sanjay Gupta, EMP-008)

4.3 C-201 — PROCESS GAS COMPRESSOR 1st STAGE (ZONE C — ALERT)
   Equipment: Siemens STC-GV 50/3, 250 kW
   Location: Unit-2 / Compressor House

   Vibration Trend (NDE Vertical, mm/s RMS):
   Jan-25: 3.8 | Feb-25: 3.9 | Mar-25: 4.0 | Apr-25: 4.2 | May-25: 4.5 |
   Jun-25: 4.8 | Jul-25: 5.0 | Aug-25: 5.4 | Sep-25: 5.8

   Spectrum Analysis:
   - Dominant frequencies: 1x and multiples of 1x RPM (740 CPM)
   - Harmonics suggest looseness or impact-type excitation
   - Cross-head bearing vibration normal
   - NDE vertical amplitude increasing at 0.3 mm/s per month

   Diagnosis: Suspected valve plate impact or looseness. Consistent with
   worn discharge valve plate on 1st stage. Next scheduled valve inspection
   (PM-COMP-6MONTH-001) is due in November 2025.

   RECOMMENDATION: Advance scheduled valve inspection to October 2025.
   Monitor vibration weekly until inspection. If vibration exceeds 7.1 mm/s
   before scheduled date, shut down for immediate inspection.
   Priority: MEDIUM
   Work Order Raised: WO-2025-0425 (PM schedule advanced)
